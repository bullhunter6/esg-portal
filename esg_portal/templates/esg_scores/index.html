Understanding ESG Scores

Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) scores are ratings that assess a company's performance in these three key areas:

Environmental

Measures a company's impact on the natural environment, including carbon emissions, resource usage, waste management, and climate change initiatives.

  • Carbon Footprint
  • Energy Efficiency
  • Waste Management
  • Water Usage
  • Climate Change Policies
Social

Evaluates a company's relationships with employees, suppliers, customers, and communities, including labor practices, diversity, human rights, and community engagement.

  • Labor Practices
  • Diversity & Inclusion
  • Human Rights
  • Community Relations
  • Product Safety
Governance

Assesses a company's leadership, executive compensation, audits, internal controls, shareholder rights, and transparency in reporting.

  • Board Structure
  • Executive Compensation
  • Audit & Controls
  • Shareholder Rights
  • Business Ethics

Available Rating Agencies

Our platform aggregates ESG scores from multiple leading rating agencies:

Agency Score Range Description
S&P Global 0-100 (higher is better) Comprehensive ESG scores with environmental, social, and governance components.
Sustainalytics 0-100 (lower is better) ESG risk ratings that measure a company's exposure to industry-specific material ESG risks.
ISS Letter grades (A+ to D-) Corporate ratings based on industry-specific environmental and social challenges.
LSEG 0-100 (higher is better) Transparent scoring methodology with over 450 metrics across 10 themes.
MSCI Letter grades (AAA to CCC) Measures a company's resilience to long-term ESG risks.
CDP Letter grades (A to F) Focuses on environmental impact, particularly climate change, water security, and deforestation.
